    Brief

    We were to innovate a new online experience for our clients’ customer base, integrating their current paper forms and products into a new, seamless customer experience.

    To ensure confidentality of our client's app, partial content below has been redacted, and colours have been changed.

    The dos and don'ts

    We first sat down with different shareholders – product owners, key staff, internal developers – to figure out what we could do and could not do. There were many legacy and server constraints, as well as security issues that we had to be attentive towards. The new app would be compromised if we did not design with these constraints in mind.

    Remapping the experience

    The different elements of the current experience was fragmented: paper forms, information which would be ported into the online database, online products & services.

    How can we unify these elements into a seamless experience for customers?

    We brainstormed different ideas: how we could break up the extensive onboarding form, different ways we might present our client’s products, design work-arounds for minimal disruption to customers.

    Iterate, test, Re-map, repeat

    We laid out the different elements, mapping them so we had a better visual. Then, we moved things around, imagining how it might be like as a customer to walk through this new experience. Some things worked, while others just made the interaction even more jarring. We put various flow iterations to the test with user interviews alongside the Customer Experience team. We repeatedly did this a few times to finish with a flow that we were satisfied with, before heading into the final refinements of the app’s UI.
