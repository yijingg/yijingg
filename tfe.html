    Brief

    We know nuts about funerals – yet we will have to go through one eventually. When you lose your loved one, when you are heavy-hearted and grieving, when you know nothing about managing funerals –

    What happens then?

    Learning about funerals

    Deathcare, funerals, afterlife services are rarely an open conversation in Singapore. To gain a better understanding, we started with learning about the industry by speaking with industry experts, elderly, and people familiar with the service.

    Zooming in on key areas

    We charted out a service map of Deathcare, and identified key touchpoints & painpoints in the journey. Zooming in on key painpoints within the journey that will be integral to our customers, we identified 2 main areas that were key for customers: a communicative website and supporting brand collaterals.

    Communicative website

    A key insight in our research was that Deathcare websites needed to be informative and transparent. Customers did not have expert knowledge on funeral services, but more often than not Deathcare websites were not forthcoming with information. Such websites do not build trust with customers, but only add on to confusion and anxiety.

    With these key principles in mind, we re-organised information on website, structing funeral packages by practical needs (type of burial) versus arbituary standards of package quality and superiority.

    We also addressed knowledge gaps with information about funerals, and what comes after.

We designed a carbon-copy receipt book for both parties for easy tracking of items, mitigating potential miscommunication and disagreement.
