    Brief

    We were tasked to improve our client’s current internal app. We first set out to research, interview and plot out a staff’s journey map. Then, we identified key instances in their job, and proposed how the improved app could enhance and assist their day-to-day.

    To ensure confidentality of our client's internal app, partial content below has been redacted.

    User Research

    We started out with user research. We interviewed 10 staff – from new staff members to senior staff in management roles. This way, we would be able to get a clear sensing of the varied day-to-day & their respective needs.

    Journey Mapping

    We took down key pointers on post-its during each interview, and did analysis via Affinity Mapping. We then plotted them on a timeline, to get a visual idea of what a staff’s typical journey may look like.

    Aligning goals

    With a clearer visual of what a day-to-day looks like, along with key points that were identified during interviews, we sat down with our clients to revise the app’s flow. With inputs from both business stakeholders, researchers and designers, we managed to keep a good balance between business goals & user needs. This meeting also allowed us to understand any constraints from IT (e.g. legacy issues, server loading) and re-design accordingly.

    App Redesign

    The app is now undergoing the redesign and development in stages. Unfortunately, I am unable to showcase the app online, but I will be happy to discuss it.
